25 Timpani

The timpani generally doubles the bass in a tutti chord, although many nineteenth-century composers would settle for any chord-tone — or even a dissonance — if tuning practicalities prevented them from doubling the bass. Examples abound of passages like this one from Verdi’s Simon Boccanegra (p. 260-262), where timpanis tuned to C and G play beneath the chords of D♭ minor, D, E♭, and E:

This, however, is not ideal. Nor is it necessary in the modern age of rapid tuning. Unless a dissonance is intended, today’s composers should relegate themselves to writing consonances in the timpani even when otherwise imitating the idioms of the nineteenth century.
