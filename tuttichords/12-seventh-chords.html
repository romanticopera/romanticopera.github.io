When the seventh would create an interval of a second or seventh with the voice in the highest register, it is common to omit it. This happens most often when the voice is singing the root of the chord. Dissonance & Seventh Chords

Just as a major chord is most consonant when it is made up of consonant intervals, so a diminished chord is most dissonant when it is made up of dissonant intervals. Composers often seek to emphasize this dissonance by giving to the woodwind pairs tritones or diminished sevenths:
