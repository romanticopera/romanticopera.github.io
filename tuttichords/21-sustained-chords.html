21 Sustained Chords

Like the other sections of the orchestra, the strings typically play complete chords in the correct inversion when participating in a tutti chord. The upper strings usually play double-stops or divide to create a thicker and fuller sound, while the violoncelli and contrabasses play the bass-note in unison (sounding as octaves):

In taller chords, the strings cannot fill in all the chord-tones without dividing into three or more parts. This, however, is almost never done. It is generally better to leave gaps in the string section for the other members of the orchestra to fill.

Ideally, no gap in the strings should exceed the range of an octave unless it involves the bass.

The strings typically do not exceed the range of the other instruments of the orchestra except in the lowest register, where the contrabasses frequently play an octave lower than the rest of the orchestra.
