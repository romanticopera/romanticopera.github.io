This chord, which comes directly from the overture to Weber’s Der Freischutz (p. 19), exhibits several properties found in tutti chords written for standard Romantic orchestras:

  • The flutes, clarinets, and oboes fill in all the chord-tones in the upper register with a minimal amount of doubling
  • The horns fill in the chord-tones directly below the flutes, clarinets, and oboes
  • The flutes play the highest notes of the chord
  • The bassoons play the lowest notes

The Octave

Frequently seen in the trumpets and bassoons, the octave, for whatever reason, is often avoided in the higher woodwinds. When it does appear, it is usually in the oboes.

Larger Orchestras & Doubling
