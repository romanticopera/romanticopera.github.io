28 Tapering

Tapering involves gradually diminishing a tutti chord by having instruments drop out one by one or in groups. Any time an instrument drops out of a tutti, it must do so without leaving a gap. This means that the departing instrument must meet one of the following criteria:

  1. it is playing the top note of the chord

  2. it is playing the bottom note of the chord

  3. it is doubling another instrument that continues to play after the first instrument drops out

Instruments can drop out as a group, as in c, as long as they all meet one of the three conditions together.

When instruments drop out from the highest register, it is ideal but not required for the top pitch of the orchestra to remain the same. This usually entails having a whole octave’s worth of instruments drop out at the same time:
