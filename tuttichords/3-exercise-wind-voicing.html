Answers:

  1. There is a gap between the horns and woodwinds. Either a clarinet or one of the horns should play the missing G. Also, if any chord-tones are doubled in a root-position triad, the root and third of the chord should be prioritized over the fifth.

  2. There are perfect fourths in the flutes and oboes and an octave in the clarinet. The perfect fourth in the flutes should probably remain, because flutes are most suited to playing in the highest register, but the oboe and clarinet parts should be rearranged.

  3. The flutes are playing below the oboes and clarinets, and the first oboe is playing well outside its comfortable range. The top two notes should instead be given to the flutes.
