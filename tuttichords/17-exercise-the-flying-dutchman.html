17 Exercise: The Flying Dutchman

Orchestrate the following passage from Wagner’s The Flying Dutchman (p. 402) using only tutti chords. The orchestral rhythm and harmonies are located beneath the vocal line.

The orchestra consists of:

  • 1 Piccolo
  • 2 Flutes
  • 2 Oboes
  • 2 Clarinets in B-flat
  • 2 Bassoons
  • 2 Horns in G
  • 2 Horns in D

Beginning with the third chord, the orchestra is joined by:

  • 2 Trumpets in D
  • 3 Trombones
  • 1 Tuba

Note:

  • Wagner does not exclusively treat his bassoons as bass instruments. In fact, the bassoons consistently play above the D horns in this passage.
  • Wagner tends to avoid chromatic notes in the horns. As such, none of them plays a C♯ in this passage.

With these things in mind, try orchestrating the above passage. When you are finished, compare your work to Wagner’s own orchestration below.
