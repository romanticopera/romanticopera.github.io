8 First-Inversion Triads

First-inversion triads typically omit the bass-note from the highest register, and often from the middle register as well:

The bass note is less likely to be omitted in chords functioning as predominants (ii6, I6, etc.), especially if the predominant is immediately followed by a dominant or cadential I6/4

First-inversion triads that begin or end a phrase are more likely to omit the bass note:

Doubling

The fifth of a first-inversion triad is more likely to be doubled than it is in a root-position triad. In fact, it is not uncommon for the fifth to be doubled more times than the root.

Perfect Fourths

Unlike in root-position triads, where perfect fourths detract from a chord’s consonance, the woodwind pairs frequently play perfect fourths in a first-inversion triad in order to emphasize the chord’s instability.
