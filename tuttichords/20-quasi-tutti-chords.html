20 Quasi-Tutti Chords

Quasi-tutti chords are smaller in nature than true tutti chords. They are usually found in chamber-like textures, where they emphasize and accentuate part of a musical phrase in a manner similar to a sforzando.

In a quasi-tutti chord,

  • Most of the woodwinds take part
  • Some or all of the strings take part
  • The trumpets and trombones do not take part
  • The harmony is sustained for one bar or less
  • The dynamic is softer than a double forte
  • The musical activity does not stop during the quasi-tutti

In a quasi-tutti, some of the principles governing tutti chords are relaxed. This is especially true in taller chords, where gaps are common due to the smaller number of instruments involved.
