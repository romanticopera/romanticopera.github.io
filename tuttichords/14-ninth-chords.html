14 Ninth Chords

Ninth chords are almost always found in root position with the root of the chord omitted from the higher registers. Failing to omit the root creates a tone-cluster with the seventh and ninth, something that most composers seek to avoid:

Note the following properties:

  • The ninth is the top note of the chord
  • The root is omitted from all but the lowest registers

It is also typical for the ninth to move down to the dominant before the harmony changes, as is in this example.

First, note the two-note gap in the middle register, just as there was a one-note gap in the previous example from Genoveva. Neither of the missing notes is to be found in the string section. They are simply missing.

As for the chord voicing, one can immediately recognize its unique characteristics from the reduction, which shows how the root of the ninth chord, normally omitted from the middle register, is crunched between the seventh and ninth of the chord. This is what most composers seek to avoid. The resulting dissonance is quite striking, to say the least. Whether one judges it a success or a failure is largely a matter of personal taste.
